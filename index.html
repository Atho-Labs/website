Network Snapshot

  • Payments-first mission Atho is focused on moving value cleanly, not on maintaining extra economic participation layers.
  • Public by default, private on demand Standard UTXO transfers and Platinum Shield private flows share one chain and one wallet story.
  • Deterministic policy Rewards start at 100 ATHO, decay once per day across 720 blocks, and settle into a fixed 1B ATHO cap.
  • Readable operating surface Clear docs, simple wallet roles, and fewer moving parts make the chain easier to audit and launch.
What changed

The active website now highlights the current Atho baseline instead of older reward and inflation marketing pages.

Core Network

Atho keeps the stack narrow on purpose.

The design goal is not to do everything. It is to keep payments, privacy, signatures, and policy readable enough that users, operators, and partners can understand what the chain is doing without wading through noise.

Built for real payment flow

  • Public to public Use Atho like a straightforward proof-of-work payment chain for day-to-day transfers and merchant-style settlement.
  • Public to private Move value into the privacy lane when the payment context calls for less visible transaction history.
  • Private to private and back out Private notes stay useful without locking users into a separate network or a separate wallet experience.

Why the site was simplified

  • The product is cleaner now The site should reflect Atho as a payment chain, not as a bundle of retired token mechanics.
  • The important numbers are stable Two-minute blocks, six confirmations, 100 ATHO starting reward, daily decay, and a one-billion ATHO cap.
  • The story now fits the mission Public payments, private transactions, post-quantum signatures, and operator clarity are the high points that matter.

Platinum Shield keeps privacy inside the payment experience.

Atho does not split privacy off into a different product identity. Public transfers stay available, private transfers stay native, and the wallet can move between both without forcing users to learn a second network.

Privacy guardrails

  • Bundle and X-address binding Private receive identity is explicit and wallet-readable.
  • Nullifier replay protection Private notes cannot be spent twice without failing validation.
  • Fail-closed validation Consensus rejects malformed private flows instead of trying to guess user intent.
