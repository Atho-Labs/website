Core Network

Atho breaks into auditable modules.

Start with the six live modules below. Each one maps to a current page or runbook instead of a generic marketing claim.

How Atho moves value

  • Public to public Use Atho for direct transfers, treasury movement, and merchant-style settlement.
  • Public to private Move into privacy when a payment needs less visible history or cleaner recipient separation.
  • Private to private and back out Private value stays usable without leaving the network or abandoning standard settlement.

Why this model works

  • One chain, not a maze Public and private movement share the same network, wallet model, and confirmation rules.
  • Policy you can verify Issuance, maturity, fees, and supply limits are short enough to remember and clear enough to audit.
  • Built for long-horizon trust Post-quantum signing, deterministic recovery, and fixed supply give Atho a stronger long-term foundation.

Platinum Shield keeps privacy in the payment flow.

Public transfers stay available, private transfers stay native, and the wallet moves between both without pushing users onto a second network or separate custody model.

Privacy guardrails

  • Bundle and X-address binding Private receive identity is explicit and wallet-readable.
  • Nullifier replay protection Private notes cannot be spent twice without failing validation.
  • Fail-closed validation Consensus rejects malformed private flows instead of trying to guess user intent.
